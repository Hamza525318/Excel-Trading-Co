JCBC Bolt — Joint Connector Bolt

High Tensile  ·  Black Oxide & Galvanized Finish  ·  Hex Socket Drive  ·  M4 to M10

The JCBC Bolt (Joint Connector Bolt) is a high tensile machine bolt with a flat countersunk head and hex socket (Allen key) drive, used to join two furniture panels at a right angle. The bolt passes through a clearance hole in one panel and threads into a barrel nut (cross dowel) fitted into the adjoining panel, drawing the joint together with a clean, flush finish. It is one of the most widely used connectors in knock-down (KD) and flat-pack furniture assembly. Excel Trading Corporation stocks this in black oxide and galvanized (zinc plated) finish.

Installation Tips

  • ▸ Always pair the JCBC bolt with a matching barrel nut — driving it directly into bare wood is not recommended for load-bearing furniture joints.
  • ▸ Select a length that fully engages the barrel nut without bottoming out — too short and the joint won't tighten; too long and the bolt tip can protrude past the barrel nut.
  • ▸ Use the correct size hex key for the socket drive — an undersized key rounds out the socket and makes the bolt difficult to remove later.
  • ▸ Tighten firmly but avoid overtightening into softwood or MDF, which can strip the barrel nut's grip on the panel.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a JCBC bolt (joint connector bolt)?

A JCBC bolt is a high tensile machine bolt with a flat countersunk head and a hex socket (Allen key) drive, designed to join two furniture panels at a right angle. The bolt passes through a clearance hole in one panel and threads into a barrel nut (cross dowel) fitted into a perpendicular hole in the adjoining panel, drawing the two panels tightly together. It is one of the most common connectors used in knock-down (KD) and flat-pack furniture because the flush head leaves a clean, low-profile finish.

Do I need a barrel nut to use a JCBC bolt, or can it be used alone?

A JCBC bolt is designed to be used together with a barrel nut — the bolt threads into the barrel nut rather than into a tapped hole in wood, which would strip out over repeated assembly and disassembly. Excel Trading Corporation stocks matching barrel nuts alongside the JCBC bolt for exactly this pairing. Using the bolt without a barrel nut into bare wood is not recommended for load-bearing furniture joints.

What is the difference between the black oxide and galvanized finish?

Black oxide is a thin conversion coating that gives a matte black appearance and mild corrosion resistance — the standard choice for visible furniture hardware where a dark, low-glare finish is preferred. Galvanized (zinc plated) gives a bright silver finish with better corrosion resistance for humid environments or lighter-coloured furniture. Choose based on the finish that matches your furniture hardware and the environment it will be used in.

Why are M4 and M5 JCBC bolts a different grade to the larger sizes?

M4 and M5 JCBC bolts are supplied in Grade 10.9, a higher tensile grade than the standard high tensile (HT) specification used for M6, M8 and M10. Smaller diameter bolts have a proportionally smaller cross-section carrying the same type of joint load, so a higher grade steel is used to maintain adequate strength margins at the smaller sizes. All sizes are suitable for standard furniture assembly loads within their rated diameter.
