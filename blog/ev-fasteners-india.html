Industry Insight By Excel Trading Corporation · 5–6 min read · EV · Automotive · Infrastructure

India’s fasteners market is entering a high-growth phase, driven by electric vehicles, infrastructure expansion and domestic manufacturing initiatives. According to industry estimates, the market reached approximately USD 11.2 billion in 2025 and is projected to grow to nearly USD 17 billion by 2034, at a steady CAGR of around 4.5–5%.

The biggest shift? Electric vehicles (EVs). As EV production scales rapidly across India, the demand for lightweight, high-strength and corrosion-resistant fasteners is increasing just as fast.

For OEMs, suppliers and procurement teams, this is not just growth — it’s a shift in what types of fasteners are in demand. Here are five categories that are seeing the strongest traction in India’s EV ecosystem right now.

1. Lightweight Aluminum & Titanium Bolts

Weight reduction is critical in EV design. Lower weight directly improves battery efficiency and vehicle range. This has led to a growing shift toward aluminum and titanium fasteners in place of traditional steel in select applications.

These fasteners can reduce overall component weight significantly while maintaining strength where required. In EV platforms, even a small reduction per component adds up across the full assembly.

Industry Insight: Lightweight materials are seeing adoption across EV platforms globally, with Indian manufacturers increasingly integrating them into battery packs, chassis components and enclosures.

Typical Applications: Battery enclosures, structural brackets, motor housings

Common Sizes: M6 to M12

2. SS316 & Coated Fasteners for Corrosion Resistance

India’s climate — from coastal humidity to monsoon exposure — makes corrosion resistance a key requirement, especially for EV battery systems and underbody assemblies.

Stainless Steel 316 (SS316) fasteners and specially coated fasteners are increasingly preferred due to their ability to resist rust, chemical exposure and moisture-related degradation.

This is particularly important in EV battery packs, where long-term durability directly impacts safety and performance.

Why it matters: Corrosion-related failures in battery mounts and enclosures can lead to structural instability. Using SS316 fasteners significantly improves lifecycle reliability.

Typical Applications: Battery packs, external assemblies, solar structures

3. High-Precision Threaded Fasteners (Automation-Ready)

As India’s automotive sector moves toward automation and localization, precision fasteners are becoming essential. EV manufacturing lines increasingly rely on automated assembly systems, where consistency and thread accuracy are critical.

High-precision screws and bolts ensure smooth integration with torque tools, robotic assembly and quality control systems.

With initiatives encouraging domestic manufacturing, Indian suppliers are seeing higher demand for tight-tolerance, high-repeatability fasteners.

Typical Sizes: M6 to M20

Key Requirement: Consistent threading and torque performance

4. Fasteners for Renewable Energy & EV Infrastructure

The EV boom is not just about vehicles — it extends to charging infrastructure, solar energy systems and smart city projects. These applications require durable fasteners that can withstand outdoor conditions over long periods.

Fasteners used in solar panel mounting systems and EV charging stations typically require high tensile strength (Grade 8.8 and above) along with corrosion resistance.

As India expands its renewable energy capacity, this segment is becoming a major driver of fastener demand.

Growth Driver: Government-backed infrastructure projects and renewable energy expansion are creating steady, long-term demand for industrial fasteners.

5. Custom Fasteners for EV Applications

Standard fasteners are not always enough in EV manufacturing. Many OEMs require custom-designed fasteners tailored to specific torque, load and space constraints.

This includes modified heads, special coatings, unique threading and application-specific geometries.

As EV designs evolve, the need for customization is increasing — especially in battery modules, lightweight assemblies and compact systems.

Real-World Insight: Custom fasteners can improve torque efficiency and assembly reliability, especially in high-performance EV components.

What This Means for Buyers & Manufacturers

The shift toward EVs is not just increasing demand — it is changing the type of fasteners required. Buyers are no longer selecting purely based on cost, but also on performance, weight, durability and compatibility with automated systems.

For manufacturers and procurement teams, this means:

  • Prioritizing lightweight and high-strength materials
  • Ensuring corrosion resistance for Indian conditions
  • Adopting precision fasteners for automated assembly
  • Exploring custom solutions for EV-specific designs
